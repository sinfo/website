Marco Squarcina - Breaking the (Web Security) Boundaries: Related-Domain Attackers in the Modern Web

Is your website secure? Whom are you protecting from? What are you doing to help researchers disclosing vulnerabilities found on your site? Would you fix the reported issues? In this talk, I will explore threat modeling failures by reporting on security issues posed by attackers who control a sibling domain of their target web application, e.g., as the result of a subdomain takeover. I will present the different attack vectors that such attackers can abuse to acquire a privileged position and discuss the impact on web application security. The results shown in this talk are based on a large-scale security measurement that we conducted on the top 50k sites,which led to the discovery of vulnerabilities in thousands of applications. I will also summarize the outcome of our vulnerability disclosure campaign, highlighting its challenges and the remediation rate on affected websites 6 months after the first notification.
